Beer Recipes and Styles

Derek Pickern

04.17.2018

Abstract

I explore homebrew beer recipes and the context of styles and ingredients. My goal is to explore patterns in beer styles to better understand how styles of beers are trending and how our palettes are changing.

Introduction

Kaggle.com has a wide variety of datasets for public consumption (mostly public, there are some private datasets). The beer data was downloaded from: https://www.kaggle.com/jtrofe/beer-recipes/data. The data consists of over 75,000 homebrew recipes from Brewers Friend. This data was initially in two separate .csvs. One .csv for recipe information (Name, Size, Original Gravity), the other for beer-style (Lager, Ale, Stout, etc). The two sets were merged on StyleID.

Beer and IBUs

Beer and ABV

ABV is the measurement of Alcohol by Volume. ABV is measured in % and indicates what portion of the beer is alcohol. A higher ABV the stronger the beer. Of the 73,861 recipes we can see there is a pretty normal looking distribution. In the below plot the ABV has been limited to shorten the right tail.
